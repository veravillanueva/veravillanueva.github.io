OVERVIEW

I live near the Yale University Art Gallery (YUAG) and often visit the museum for both work and fun. I have long felt that some parts of the YUAG's mobile app are a bit hard to navigate, so I performed an unsolicited redesign of the UI to see if I could make it more accessible. Since I don’t have access to user data or research about the app’s current design, I relied on my own experience as a frequent visitor to the museum and did my best to maintain the overall feel of the app.

Language Options

PROBLEM

The current language options are buried under layers of menus. This is an issue for the many international tourists who visit the gallery every day.

SOLUTION

I inserted a screen immediately before the home page as a one-time prompt to allow users to set a language before using the app. If they accidentally choose the wrong language at the outset, no worries! The language options are easily accessible at the top of the home page.

Audio


The original UI does not let users listen to audio and browse other parts of the app at the same time. I redesigned the interface to allow audio pages to be minimized and added a dark background in order to draw less attention to the screen and emphasize the central image.

Permanent Collection


There is currently no way to access the images and descriptions of the YUAG’s permanent exhibition, which constitutes the majority of the gallery’s collection. I made sure to include them in my redesign.

Special Exhibitions

PROBLEM

Descriptions of special exhibitions, a main attraction of the museum, are virtually non-existent in the original UI. There is only a link to the gallery’s website, which is poorly adapted for phone screens.

SOLUTION

I included links to both special exhibitions and permanent exhibitions on the home page and developed a template to showcase specific collections of work.
